Brand story

Not a souvenir shop. Not an antique dealer.

This concept brings Nepali craftsmanship into contemporary German homes through tasteful presentation, honest labeling, and a mix of giftable, decorative, and premium pieces.

The focus is on modern artisan goods, vintage-style décor, textiles, prayer wheels, thangka reproductions, carved furniture, brassware, and clearly described handmade products.

Why customers will remember it

  • A warm visual identity that feels calm, elevated, and welcoming.
  • Clear product language with no confusing “antique” claims.
  • A balance of small impulse gifts and larger statement pieces.
  • An ethical, transparent sourcing story that builds trust.
Transparent sourcing

How the collection is selected

Everything is presented as contemporary handicraft, decorative object, or clearly labeled reproduction, with a focus on cultural respect and traceability.

What we focus on

Modern artisan goods, decorative home pieces, textiles, and well-documented handcrafted furniture.

What we avoid

Undocumented antiques, archaeological-style pieces, and unclear sacred or heritage objects.

What this means

Better trust, better margins, and fewer legal headaches — while keeping the collection beautiful and authentic.
